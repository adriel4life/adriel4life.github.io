What Makes Nu-Metal Different?

Nu-metal is a style of alternative metal that became especially popular in the late 1990s and early 2000s. The genre mixes heavy guitar riffs with influences from hip-hop, grunge, industrial music, funk, and electronic music. Many songs focus on personal struggle, anger, isolation, identity, and emotional release.

Common Features

Heavy Riffs

Guitars are often low-tuned, rhythmic, and focused on groove instead of long solos.

Rap Influence

Some bands use rapped verses, turntables, spoken rhythm, or hip-hop production ideas.

Emotional Lyrics

Lyrics often discuss pain, pressure, confusion, anger, and feeling misunderstood.

Visual Identity

Fashion, masks, album art, and live-show energy are major parts of the culture.
