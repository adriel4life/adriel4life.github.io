Popular Bands

These artists were revolutionary to nu-metal by combining distorted guitars with hip-hop elements, driving vocals, and personal lyrics.

Slipknot

Slipknot became known for chaotic live performances, horror-inspired masks, and a sound built around aggression, percussion, and dark emotion. Their music has often been misunderstood, but their influence on nu-metal was very theatrical and unforgettable.

Linkin Park

Linkin Park was one of the bands that blended rock, rap, and EDM the most. In my personal opinion, their music mostly reaches listeners who connect with themes like frustration, pressure, and healing.

Korn

Korn is often seen as one of the pioneering bands of nu-metal. Their low-tuned guitars, unusual bass tone, and raw energy on stage really pioneered that darker and more personal style of heavy music.

Deftones

Perhaps one of the most well-known nu-metal bands, Deftones, brought a more atmospheric side to heavy music. Their songs alternate between quiet, dreamy sections and heavy, distorted moments, giving the genre more emotional range. However, since the 90s, their sound has often become more atmospheric and experimental over time.

Limp Bizkit

Limp Bizkit was another one of those nu-metal bands that integrated rap-rock and catchy hooks into their music. In my personal opinion, their music, especially during the 2000s, shows how the genre crossed into mainstream pop culture.
